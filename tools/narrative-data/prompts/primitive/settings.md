You are helping build a multidimensional setting model for a narrative engine. Your task is to produce a rich, genre-agnostic analysis of a single setting type: **{target_name}**.

This analysis will serve as the setting's standalone description — the canonical reference that the engine uses before applying any genre-specific modulation. It must be detailed enough to instantiate this setting in any genre context, while remaining genuinely genre-neutral.

## Atmospheric Signature

Describe the characteristic mood and emotional quality this setting creates by its nature, independent of what happens within it:

- **Baseline mood** — what emotional state does mere presence in this setting generate? Comfort, unease, wonder, claustrophobia, expansiveness, melancholy, anticipation? What is the default emotional register before any narrative events occur?
- **Mood range** — how far can the mood deviate from baseline while the setting remains recognizable? What is the brightest and darkest this setting can go? What shifts the mood — time of day, weather, occupancy, state of repair?
- **Atmospheric mechanics** — what specific physical properties create the mood? Is it the scale, the light quality, the enclosure, the materials, the sounds, the emptiness, the density? How do these properties interact to produce the emotional experience?
- **Subliminal effects** — what does this setting do to characters psychologically just by being in it? Does it make people more honest, more guarded, more vulnerable, more aggressive? What about the space itself shapes human behavior?

## Sensory Palette

Describe the characteristic sensory experience of this setting across all channels:

- **Visual** — what does this setting look like? What is the quality of light? What textures, colors, and spatial geometries define the visual experience? What visual details are characteristic and which are conspicuously absent?
- **Auditory** — what does this setting sound like? What is the soundscape — silence, ambient noise, echoes, natural sounds, mechanical sounds? What sounds are characteristic? What is the quality of silence if silence is present?
- **Tactile** — what does this setting feel like physically? Temperature, humidity, surface textures, air movement, ground underfoot? What tactile details define the experience of being embodied in this space?
- **Olfactory** — what does this setting smell like? What scents are characteristic? Are they pleasant, neutral, or disturbing? How do scents change with time, season, or use?
- **Proprioceptive** — how does the body feel in this setting? Is there a sense of openness or compression, elevation or descent, stability or precariousness? How does the spatial geometry affect the physical experience of moving through it?

## Temporal Character

How does time behave in this setting?

- **Time feel** — does time move quickly, slowly, or not at all in this setting? Is there a sense of urgency, timelessness, decay, or renewal? What creates the temporal experience?
- **Temporal layers** — does this setting carry visible evidence of its past? Does it show age, renovation, accumulation, or erasure? How do temporal layers affect the experience of the present?
- **Seasonal or cyclical quality** — does this setting change with time of day, season, or other cycles? What is the characteristic version (dawn vs. midnight, summer vs. winter)?
- **Durability** — is this setting permanent, transient, or somewhere between? Does it feel like it has always been here, or like it could disappear?

## Narrative Function

What dramatic work does this setting perform? Go beyond "backdrop" into active participation:

- **Scene enablement** — what kinds of scenes does this setting naturally support? What dramatic situations does the space itself make possible or likely? What scenes would be structurally impossible in this setting?
- **Character effects** — how does this setting change the characters who inhabit it? Does it strip away pretense, amplify conflict, create intimacy, enforce hierarchy, or enable escape? What does the setting do to relationships between characters?
- **Constraint and affordance** — what does the setting's physical structure permit and forbid? Can characters hide, flee, confront, overhear, be trapped? How does the spatial geometry create dramatic possibility?
- **Symbolic capacity** — what can this setting represent? What psychological states, thematic concerns, or narrative conditions does it naturally externalize? Is the symbolic potential fixed or flexible?

## Communicability Dimensions

Describe how this setting communicates with characters and audience along each channel:

- **Atmospheric communicability** — what emotional information does this setting broadcast? How strongly does it signal mood, tone, and threat level? Is the atmospheric signal constant or variable?
- **Sensory communicability** — what information does this setting provide through sensory channels that affects character knowledge and decision-making? What can be seen, heard, smelled, or felt that matters narratively?
- **Spatial communicability** — how does the geometry and navigability of this setting shape what is possible? What does the spatial structure communicate about power, safety, freedom, and constraint?
- **Temporal communicability** — how does this setting communicate temporal information? Does it signal urgency, permanence, decay, or cyclical return? How does the setting's relationship to time affect the characters' sense of narrative position?

## Relationship to Characters

How do characters relate to this setting type?

- **Ownership and belonging** — who belongs in this setting? Whose space is it? What does it mean to be a native versus an intruder? How does the setting treat those who belong versus those who don't?
- **Arrival and departure** — how do characters typically enter and leave this setting? Is arrival dramatic or mundane? Is departure easy, difficult, or impossible? What does crossing the threshold mean?
- **Habitation effects** — what happens to characters who stay in this setting for extended periods? Does it change them? Does it become part of their identity? Can they leave it behind?

## Genre-Agnostic Invariants

What is always true about this setting regardless of genre context? These are the properties that survive transplantation — the core spatial-atmospheric identity that persists whether this setting appears in space opera, literary fiction, folk horror, or cozy mystery. Be specific: not "it is atmospheric" but "it always creates X spatial experience, communicates Y through its physical properties, and enables Z kinds of dramatic situations."

## What Flexes

What changes about this setting depending on context? Which dimensions are most sensitive to genre modulation? Which sensory details are genre-dependent? Which narrative functions shift?

Be specific about the range of variation. If Atmospheric Signature flexes from welcoming to threatening depending on genre, say that. If Spatial Orientation is always enclosed regardless of context, say that too. The goal is to identify the setting's fixed spatial-atmospheric core versus its contextual surface.

Be expansive. This is exploratory research, not a database entry.
