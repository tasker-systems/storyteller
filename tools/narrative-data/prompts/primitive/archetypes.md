You are helping build a multidimensional character archetype model for a narrative engine. Your task is to produce a rich, genre-agnostic analysis of a single archetype: **{target_name}**.

This analysis will serve as the archetype's standalone description — the canonical reference that the engine uses before applying any genre-specific modulation. It must be detailed enough to instantiate a character from this archetype in any genre context, while remaining genuinely genre-neutral.

## Personality Axes

Analyze where this archetype typically sits on each dimension. For each axis, do not just state a position — explain why this archetype gravitates there, what pressures or narrative functions push it to that point, and under what conditions it might deviate.

- **Warmth** (cold detachment <-> deep emotional investment) — what is this archetype's characteristic relationship to emotional connection? Is warmth a tool, a vulnerability, a defining trait, or something they resist?

- **Openness** (guarded/secretive <-> transparent/forthcoming) — how does this archetype relate to information? Do they hoard knowledge, share freely, perform transparency while concealing, or genuinely lack hidden depths?

- **Agency** (reactive/passive <-> proactive/driving) — does this archetype initiate or respond? Do they shape events or get shaped by them? Is their agency situational — high in their domain, low outside it?

- **Morality** (ruthless pragmatism <-> principled idealism) — what is this archetype's moral stance? Is it fixed or contextual? Do they have a code, and if so, what breaks it?

- **Stability** (volatile/unpredictable <-> steady/anchored) — how consistent is this archetype's behavior? What destabilizes them? What grounds them? Is their stability genuine or performed?

- **Authority** (deferential/peripheral <-> commanding/central) — where does this archetype sit in social hierarchies? Is their authority formal, earned, assumed, or resisted? How do they respond to authority in others?

- **Interiority** (opaque/action-defined <-> rich inner life visible to the reader) — how much access does the audience get to this archetype's inner world? Is their opacity deliberate or structural? What makes their interiority narratively important?

## Behavioral Patterns

How does this archetype act in scenes? Go beyond personality description into observable behavior:

- **Speech registers** — how does this archetype talk? Formal/informal, verbose/terse, direct/oblique, honest/performative? Do they have characteristic rhetorical moves (deflection through humor, authority through silence, intimacy through confession)?
- **Action tendencies** — what does this archetype do when given a choice? When threatened? When bored? When presented with an opportunity? What actions are characteristic and which are conspicuously absent?
- **Relationship to objects and space** — how does this archetype inhabit physical settings? Do they dominate rooms, shrink into corners, rearrange furniture, notice exits? What objects do they gravitate toward or avoid?
- **Characteristic entrances and exits** — how does this archetype typically arrive in and leave scenes? This reveals a surprising amount about narrative function.

## Narrative Roles and Functions

What dramatic purpose does this archetype serve? A single archetype may serve multiple functions depending on story position:

- **Structural roles** — catalyst, obstacle, mirror, threshold guardian, herald, shadow, mentor, trickster, shapeshifter, ally? Which of these does the archetype most naturally fill, and which would be surprising?
- **Story-phase affinity** — at what point in a story does this archetype typically appear or become important? Early stories need different characters than midpoints or climaxes.
- **Dramatic promises** — what does the audience expect when this archetype appears? What narrative trajectories become likely? What does the audience anticipate wanting from this character?
- **Transformation potential** — is this archetype typically static or dynamic? If dynamic, what is the characteristic arc? What triggers transformation, and what does the transformed version look like?

## Relationship Tendencies

How does this archetype relate to other characters?

- **Power dynamics** — does this archetype seek power over others, submit to it, negotiate it, ignore it, or subvert it? How do they behave with those above and below them?
- **Trust patterns** — how does this archetype build trust? How easily do they extend it? What betrays it? Are they trustworthy themselves?
- **Alliance and opposition** — which other archetypes does this one naturally ally with or oppose? What makes for productive tension when paired with specific other character types?
- **Intimacy and distance** — how close does this archetype let others get? Is intimacy something they offer, withhold, weaponize, or fear?

## Narrative Promise

When this archetype appears in a story, what implicit contract exists with the audience? What do readers or players expect will happen? What kinds of scenes become likely? What emotional registers become available?

The narrative promise is not the same as the plot function — it is the audience's felt sense of what this character brings to the story. A trickster promises disruption and revelation. A threshold guardian promises a test that transforms. What does this archetype promise?

## Genre-Agnostic Invariants

What is always true about this archetype regardless of genre context? These are the properties that survive transplantation — the core identity that persists whether this character appears in space opera, literary fiction, folk horror, or cozy mystery. Be specific: not "they are complex" but "they always carry a tension between X and Y that manifests as Z."

## What Flexes

What changes about this archetype depending on context? Which personality axes are most sensitive to genre modulation? Which behavioral patterns are genre-dependent? Which narrative functions shift?

Be specific about the range of variation. If Warmth flexes from moderate-low to moderate-high depending on genre, say that. If Agency is always high regardless of context, say that too. The goal is to identify the archetype's fixed core versus its contextual surface.

Be expansive. This is exploratory research, not a database entry.
