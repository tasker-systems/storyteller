## Role Definitions

Describe the two roles that constitute this dynamic. For each role, go beyond a label into the structural position:

- **Role A** and **Role B** — name and describe each role. What does each role characteristically want, offer, withhold, and fear within this dynamic? What is each role's relationship to power, information, and emotional investment? How does each role's behavior sustain the dynamic?

- **Asymmetry or symmetry** — is this a dynamic between equals, or does it have an inherent tilt? If asymmetric, which role holds more power, and what kind of power? Is the asymmetry stable, or does the dynamic tend to invert? If symmetric, what maintains the balance — mutual respect, mutual destruction, or mutual need?

- **Role fluidity** — can the same character occupy both roles at different points in a story? Under what conditions does role-switching occur? What does it mean for the dynamic when roles reverse?

## Relational Texture

Analyze the characteristic quality of interaction along each dimension. For each axis, do not just state a position — explain why this dynamic gravitates there, what pressures sustain it, and under what conditions it might shift.

- **Power asymmetry** (balanced <-> steep hierarchy) — how is power distributed between the two roles? Is it formal, earned, seized, or structural? How does power shift within the dynamic over time?

- **Volatility** (stable/predictable <-> explosive/unstable) — how quickly can this dynamic change state? What triggers rupture, and what triggers stabilization? Is the volatility productive (generating narrative energy) or destructive (threatening the dynamic's existence)?

- **Dependency** (autonomous/optional <-> enmeshed/necessary) — can either role walk away? What binds them — choice, circumstance, obligation, need, or habit? How does dependency change the power calculus?

- **Visibility** (overt/acknowledged <-> covert/denied) — do the participants recognize the dynamic they are in? Does the audience? What happens when a covert dynamic is made visible? What happens when an overt dynamic goes underground?

- **Emotional register** (cold/transactional <-> warm/intimate) — what is the characteristic temperature? Is warmth a tool, a vulnerability, or a genuine state? Can the register shift, and what triggers the shift?

- **Evolution tendency** (crystallizing/static <-> transforming/unstable) — does this dynamic tend toward a fixed state or continuous change? What is the attractor — does it settle into routine, deepen into something new, or perpetually oscillate?

## Evolution Patterns

How does this dynamic typically change over the course of a story? Go beyond a single arc into the space of possible trajectories:

- **Characteristic arc** — what is the most common evolution pattern? Deepening, erosion, inversion, crystallization, oscillation? What drives the transformation?
- **Turning points** — what are the characteristic pressure points where the dynamic could break, deepen, or transform? What kinds of events or revelations trigger these moments?
- **Terminal states** — how does this dynamic end? Does it resolve, dissolve, transform into something else, or persist indefinitely? What does each possible ending mean for the characters involved?
- **Resistance to change** — what keeps this dynamic stable when it should change? What structural forces or character investments resist transformation?

- **Resolution scenes** — what does resolution look like for this dynamic? Is resolution even possible, or is the dynamic defined by its irresolvability?

## Narrative Promise

The narrative promise is not the same as the plot function — it is the audience's felt sense of what this relationship brings to the story. A mentor/student dynamic promises growth and eventual separation. A rivals dynamic promises escalation and eventual reckoning. What does this dynamic promise?
