You are helping build a multidimensional character archetype model for a narrative engine. Your task is to describe how the archetype **{target_name}** is specifically shaped by its genre context.

## Important: Focus on the Delta

You have access to both the archetype's standalone description and the genre's dimensional description in the Additional Context sections below. **Do not repeat the standalone archetype description.** Your job is to describe what changes — what this specific genre does to this archetype that makes its expression here distinct from the genre-agnostic baseline.

Think of the standalone description as the archetype at rest. This prompt asks: what happens when you place it in this genre's gravitational field?

## Axis Shifts

For each personality axis, describe the direction and magnitude of the shift this genre imposes. Not every axis will shift — identify the ones that do and explain why.

- **Warmth** — does this genre push the archetype toward greater emotional investment or detachment? Why? What about the genre's tonal register, thematic preoccupations, or structural shapes creates this pressure?
- **Openness** — does the genre's epistemological stance or information economy change how guarded or forthcoming this archetype is?
- **Agency** — does the genre's agency model amplify or constrain this archetype's proactive tendencies?
- **Morality** — do the genre's thematic dimensions and exclusions shift this archetype's moral positioning?
- **Stability** — does the genre's temporal orientation or tonal register affect how volatile or anchored this archetype is?
- **Authority** — does the genre's locus of power change where this archetype sits in social hierarchies?
- **Interiority** — does the genre's narrative conventions and aesthetic register change how much inner life is visible?

For each shift, be specific: "Warmth drops from moderate to low because..." is more useful than "Warmth decreases."

## Affordance Effects

How do this genre's specific dimensional properties reshape the archetype?

- **Locus of power** — where power resides in this genre determines how this archetype relates to power. If power is in place, a character who seeks knowledge must seek it through place. If power is in relationships, the same archetype becomes a character who builds or destroys bonds. Describe the specific interaction.
- **Temporal orientation** — does this genre's relationship to time change the archetype's narrative arc? A character who "returns" in a cyclical genre is fundamentally different from one who "returns" in a linear genre. Does this archetype accumulate or are they perpetually renewed?
- **State variables** — which of the genre's tracked resources does this archetype interact with? Do they deplete trust, accumulate secrets, spend social capital, erode safety? How does this interaction differ from the archetype's baseline tendencies?
- **World affordances** — what does this genre's physics (magic system, technology level, violence consequences, death permanence, supernatural presence) do to this archetype's available actions and characteristic behaviors?

## Excluded Expressions

What versions of this archetype are structurally forbidden by this genre? Every genre's exclusion list constrains the space of possible characters. If the genre rejects redemptive arcs, certain archetype trajectories are impossible. If the genre demands moral ambiguity, certain clear-cut expressions are forbidden.

Be specific about what is excluded and why. This is as informative as what is permitted — it defines the archetype's shape by carving away what it cannot be in this context.

## Genre-Specific Narrative Function

What role does this archetype play in this genre's characteristic story shapes? The same archetype may serve different dramatic functions in different genres:

- What scenes does this archetype enable or require in this genre?
- What emotional registers does this archetype access specifically because of this genre's tonal contract?
- How does this archetype interact with the genre's primary structural shape (mystery, romance, tragedy, comedy, horror, quest)? Does the archetype's narrative function align with or work against the genre's dominant structure?
- What happens to this archetype at the genre's characteristic turning points or crisis moments?

## Distinctive Genre Expression

Finally, describe what is uniquely true about this archetype in this genre that is not true elsewhere. What behaviors, relationships, narrative functions, or personality configurations exist only at this intersection of archetype and genre? This is the most valuable output — the genre-specific character knowledge that cannot be derived from either the standalone archetype description or the genre description alone.

Be expansive. This is exploratory research, not a database entry.
