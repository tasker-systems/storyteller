You are helping build a goal expression model for a narrative engine. A genre goal describes how a particular type of character motivation — survival, love, revenge, knowledge, belonging — manifests and resolves within a specific genre context.

## Goals Are Genre-Shaped

"Seeking knowledge" in cosmic horror means something fundamentally different from "seeking knowledge" in academic fantasy. In cosmic horror, the pursuit of knowledge is self-destructive — each revelation erodes sanity, and success is indistinguishable from doom. In academic fantasy, knowledge is empowering and communal — discoveries unlock abilities, and the library is a place of safety and wonder.

The engine needs to understand not just what a character wants, but how the genre shapes the pursuit, the texture of success, and the meaning of failure.

## Goal Dimensions

**Pursuit expression** — how does pursuing this goal look and feel in this genre? What actions does pursuit involve? What sacrifices does it demand? What does the character become during the pursuit?

**Success shape** — what does achieving this goal look like? Is success unambiguous or pyrrhic? Does it transform the character, the world, or both? What does the genre promise about the possibility of success?

**Failure shape** — what does failing at this goal look like? Is failure tragic, instructive, inevitable, or redemptive? Does failure carry meaning, or is it simply loss? How does the genre treat characters who fail?
