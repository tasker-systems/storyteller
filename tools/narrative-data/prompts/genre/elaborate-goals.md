You are helping build a multidimensional character goal model for a narrative engine. Your task is to describe how the character goal **{target_name}** is specifically shaped by its genre context.

## Important: Focus on the Delta

You have access to both the goal's standalone description and the genre's dimensional description in the Additional Context sections below. **Do not repeat the standalone goal description.** Your job is to describe what changes — what this specific genre does to this goal that makes its expression here distinct from the genre-agnostic baseline.

Think of the standalone description as the goal at rest. This prompt asks: what happens when you place it in this genre's gravitational field?

## Goal Dimension Shifts

For each goal dimension, describe the direction and magnitude of the shift this genre imposes. Not every dimension will shift — identify the ones that do and explain why.

- **Temporal scope** — does this genre compress or extend the timeline of pursuit? Why? What about the genre's temporal orientation creates this pressure? Does the genre demand immediate resolution, or does it allow generational striving?
- **Visibility** — does the genre's epistemological stance change whether this goal is overt or hidden? Do characters in this genre acknowledge what they want, or does the genre's relationship to self-knowledge force the goal underground?
- **Moral weight** — do the genre's thematic commitments and exclusions shift the ethical valence of pursuing this goal? Does the genre judge this pursuit more harshly or more sympathetically than the baseline?
- **Success accessibility** — does the genre's structural shape make success more or less achievable? Does the genre promise that this goal can be reached, or does it insist on asymptotic approach? What about the genre's relationship to resolution determines this?
- **Pursuit cost** — does the genre raise or lower the price of pursuit? What resources does this genre demand that the baseline does not? What sacrifices become structurally necessary in this genre's economy?
- **Social orientation** — does the genre push this goal toward individual pursuit or collective endeavor? Does the genre's agency model and locus of power change whether this goal can be pursued alone?

For each shift, be specific: "Moral Weight increases from moderate to high because..." is more useful than "Moral Weight increases."

## Affordance Effects

How do this genre's specific dimensional properties reshape the goal?

- **Locus of power** — where power resides in this genre determines what pursuing this goal requires. If power is in knowledge, the goal's pursuit involves acquiring or controlling information. If power is in relationships, pursuit requires building or destroying bonds. If power is in place, the goal may require physical journey or territorial claim. Describe the specific interaction.
- **Temporal orientation** — does this genre's relationship to time change the goal's pursuit and resolution? In cyclical genres, the goal may be pursued across iterations, each attempt informed by the last. In linear genres, there is one chance. Does the goal accumulate or is the character perpetually renewed?
- **State variables** — which of the genre's tracked resources does this goal interact with? Does pursuit deplete sanity, trust, social capital, safety, or moral standing? Does success accumulate resources that enable new pursuits, or does it spend everything? How does this interaction differ from the goal's baseline resource economy?
- **World affordances** — what does this genre's physics (magic, technology, violence consequences, death permanence, supernatural presence) do to the goal's available pursuit strategies and possible outcomes? Can this goal be achieved by means that are impossible in other genres? Are certain pursuit paths blocked?

## Excluded Expressions

What versions of this goal are structurally forbidden by this genre? Every genre's exclusion list constrains the space of possible motivations and their expressions. If the genre rejects unambiguous triumph, certain forms of success are impossible. If the genre demands moral ambiguity, certain clean-handed pursuits are forbidden.

Be specific about what is excluded and why. This is as informative as what is permitted — it defines the goal's shape by carving away what it cannot be in this context.

## Genre-Specific Pursuit Texture

How does pursuing this goal feel differently in this genre?

- What does the day-to-day texture of pursuit look like in this genre's world? What actions, sacrifices, and compromises are genre-specific?
- How does the genre's aesthetic register shape how pursuit is narrated and experienced? Is pursuit glamorous, grinding, desperate, methodical, or ecstatic?
- What does the genre's agency model mean for pursuit? Can the character pursue actively, or does the genre constrain agency in ways that make pursuit a matter of endurance rather than action?

## Genre-Specific Success and Failure

How do achievement and failure change in this genre?

- What does success look like specifically in this genre? Is it the same outcome with different emotional texture, or does the genre redefine what counts as success?
- What does failure look like specifically in this genre? Does the genre treat failure as tragedy, wisdom, natural order, or something else?
- Does the genre allow clean resolution, or does it insist on costs, complications, and ambiguity in the outcome?

## Distinctive Genre Expression

Finally, describe what is uniquely true about this goal in this genre that is not true elsewhere. What pursuit textures, success shapes, failure modes, or resource economies exist only at this intersection of goal and genre? This is the most valuable output — the genre-specific motivational knowledge that cannot be derived from either the standalone goal description or the genre description alone.

Be expansive. This is exploratory research, not a database entry.
