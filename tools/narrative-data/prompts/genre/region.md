You are helping build a multidimensional genre model for a narrative engine. A genre region is a named cluster in narrative space, defined by its position along multiple dimensional categories. Some genres are standalone regions; others function as dimensional modifiers or constraint layers applied to other regions. Part of your task is to determine which this genre is.

## Dimensional Framework

**Aesthetic dimensions** — visual and sensory vocabulary, prose register, descriptive density:
- Spare/minimalist ←→ Lush/ornate
- Grounded/mundane ←→ Heightened/mythic
- Gritty realism ←→ Lyrical beauty

**Tonal dimensions** — emotional contract with the reader/player:
- Dread ←→ Wonder
- Cynicism ←→ Earnestness
- Surface irony (dialogue, narration style) ←→ Surface sincerity
- Structural sincerity (does the emotional payoff land genuinely?) ←→ Structural irony (the payoff is undercut or hollow)
- Intimacy ←→ Epic distance

**Temporal dimensions** — how the genre relates to time:
- Linear progression ←→ Cyclical/seasonal
- Compressed urgency ←→ Expansive patience
- Mythic/timeless ←→ Historically specific
- Episodic ←→ Saga/generational

**Thematic dimensions** — what the story is about beneath its surface:
- Power and its corruption (or reframed: stewardship, negotiation, systemic pressure — describe how this genre understands power)
- Identity and belonging
- Knowledge and its cost
- Connection and isolation

**Agency dimensions** — how much control characters have over their fate:
- High agency (characters shape the world) ←→ Environmental determinism (the world shapes characters)
- Individual triumph ←→ Collective success
- Competence validated ←→ Competence irrelevant

**Locus of power** — where does power reside in this genre?
- Place (the land, the house, the city)
- Person (the hero, the leader, the genius)
- System/institution (the state, the corporation, the church)
- Relationship (the bond, the community, the family)
- Cosmos/fate (the gods, entropy, indifferent universe)

**Structural dimensions** — how the story moves, what shapes it takes. Most genres blend 2-3 structures in tension — describe which are primary and secondary, and how they interact:
- Mystery (concealment → revelation)
- Romance (separation → union)
- Tragedy (ascent → fall)
- Comedy (disorder → restored order)
- Horror (safety → violation of safety)
- Quest (lack → fulfillment or failure)

**World affordances** — what is possible in this story's physics:
- Magic: absent / subtle / rule-bound / wild-mythic / technological mimicry
- Technology: historical / contemporary / speculative / post-human
- Violence: consequence-laden / stylized / absent
- Death: permanent / negotiable / metaphorical
- The supernatural: nonexistent / ambiguous / matter-of-fact

**Epistemological stance** — the genre's relationship to knowledge and knowability:
- Knowable ←→ Unknowable
- Knowledge rewarded ←→ Knowledge punished
- Reliable narration ←→ Unreliable narration

## Your Task

Describe the genre region **{target_name}** in rich detail.

For each dimensional category, provide specific positions with explanatory notes. Go beyond simple positioning — if the dimension doesn't quite fit this genre, explain how this genre reframes or transforms it.

### Required sections:

1. **Dimensional positions** — for all categories above, with explanatory notes
2. **Exclusions** — what does this genre deliberately reject? What breaks the reader contract? What tropes, tones, or structures are forbidden?
3. **State variables** — what changes turn by turn in stories of this genre? What resources are tracked or depleted? (e.g., trust, sanity, reputation, relationship status, resources, secrets known)
4. **Genre topology** — is this a standalone genre region, or does it function as a modifier/constraint layer applied to other genres? If a modifier, which genres does it modify and how?
5. **Boundary conditions** — where does this genre blur with its neighbors? What is the minimum change that would push a story out of this genre into an adjacent one? Name specific neighboring genres and the axes along which the boundary exists.

Be expansive. This is exploratory research, not a database entry.
