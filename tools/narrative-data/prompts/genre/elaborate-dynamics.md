You are helping build a multidimensional relational dynamics model for a narrative engine. Your task is to describe how the interpersonal dynamic **{target_name}** is specifically shaped by its genre context.

## Important: Focus on the Delta

You have access to both the dynamic's standalone description and the genre's dimensional description in the Additional Context sections below. **Do not repeat the standalone dynamic description.** Your job is to describe what changes — what this specific genre does to this dynamic that makes its expression here distinct from the genre-agnostic baseline.

Think of the standalone description as the dynamic at rest. This prompt asks: what happens when you place it in this genre's gravitational field?

## Relational Dimension Shifts

For each relational dimension, describe the direction and magnitude of the shift this genre imposes. Not every dimension will shift — identify the ones that do and explain why.

- **Power asymmetry** — does this genre push the dynamic toward steeper hierarchy or greater balance? Why? What about the genre's locus of power or agency model creates this pressure?
- **Volatility** — does the genre's temporal orientation or tonal register make this dynamic more stable or more explosive? What genre properties create or suppress instability?
- **Dependency** — does the genre's structure increase or decrease the necessity of this dynamic? Are the roles bound more tightly or more loosely because of genre constraints?
- **Visibility** — does the genre's epistemological stance or information economy change whether this dynamic is acknowledged or denied? Does the genre push relationships toward transparency or concealment?
- **Emotional register** — does the genre's tonal contract raise or lower the emotional temperature of this dynamic? Does it permit warmth, demand coldness, or force a specific register?
- **Evolution tendency** — does the genre's structural shape push this dynamic toward crystallization or transformation? Does the genre allow resolution, or does it insist on perpetual tension?

For each shift, be specific: "Volatility increases from moderate to high because..." is more useful than "Volatility increases."

## Affordance Effects

How do this genre's specific dimensional properties reshape the dynamic?

- **Locus of power** — where power resides in this genre determines how the two roles relate. If power is in knowledge, the role that controls information dominates. If power is in place, proximity becomes the currency of the dynamic. If power is in lineage, the dynamic may be inherited rather than chosen. Describe the specific interaction.
- **Temporal orientation** — does this genre's relationship to time change the dynamic's evolution pattern? In cyclical genres, the dynamic may repeat and ritualize. In linear genres, each interaction is irreversible. Does the dynamic accumulate history or reset?
- **State variables** — which of the genre's tracked resources does this dynamic interact with? Does it deplete trust between the roles, accumulate mutual obligation, erode one role's safety, or build shared secrets? How does this interaction differ from the dynamic's baseline tendencies?
- **World affordances** — what does this genre's physics (magic, technology, violence consequences, death permanence, supernatural presence) do to the dynamic's available expressions? Can the roles do things to each other that are impossible in other genres?

## Excluded Expressions

What versions of this dynamic are structurally forbidden by this genre? Every genre's exclusion list constrains the space of possible relational patterns. If the genre rejects reconciliation, certain dynamic trajectories are impossible. If the genre demands moral ambiguity, certain clear-cut power relationships are forbidden.

Be specific about what is excluded and why. This is as informative as what is permitted — it defines the dynamic's shape by carving away what it cannot be in this context.

## Genre-Specific Evolution

How does this dynamic evolve differently in this genre than it would in genre-neutral form?

- What is the characteristic arc of this dynamic in this genre? How does it differ from the baseline evolution pattern?
- What genre-specific turning points or pressure points drive transformation?
- What terminal states are available or unavailable in this genre?
- How does the genre's structural shape (mystery, romance, tragedy, comedy, horror, quest) constrain or enable the dynamic's trajectory?

## Genre-Specific Scene Affordances

What scenes does this dynamic enable specifically because of this genre's tonal contract and structural shape?

- What dramatic situations become possible at the intersection of this dynamic and this genre that exist nowhere else?
- What emotional registers does this dynamic access specifically because of this genre's commitments?
- What happens to this dynamic at the genre's characteristic crisis moments?

## Distinctive Genre Expression

Finally, describe what is uniquely true about this dynamic in this genre that is not true elsewhere. What relational textures, evolution patterns, scene affordances, or power configurations exist only at this intersection of dynamic and genre? This is the most valuable output — the genre-specific relational knowledge that cannot be derived from either the standalone dynamic description or the genre description alone.

Be expansive. This is exploratory research, not a database entry.
