## What We Mean by Narrative Shape

A narrative shape is not a rigid plot outline. It is the gravitational pattern that stories follow within a genre — the characteristic arc of tension, revelation, and transformation that readers sense even when they can't name it. A mystery builds through accumulation toward revelation. A romance follows separation through escalating vulnerability toward union. A horror story tightens a noose of dread until the violation beat breaks through.

These are not formulas. They are gravitational tendencies — patterns that skilled storytellers sometimes resist, sometimes ride, and that audiences sense as the "rightness" or "wrongness" of a story's pacing.

## Beat Properties

Each beat in a narrative shape has:
- **Position** — where in the arc it typically falls (early, mid, late, climactic, denouement)
- **Flexibility** — how movable this beat is (load-bearing beats define the shape; ornamental beats enrich without constraining)
- **Tension contribution** — does this beat build, sustain, release, redirect, or transform tension?
- **Pacing function** — does this beat accelerate, decelerate, or provide breathing room? Beats that provide rest between intensity are as structurally important as the peaks.

## The Rhythm Between Peaks

A narrative shape is not just its peaks — the climaxes, revelations, and confrontations. It is equally defined by the connective tissue between peaks: the scenes of preparation, aftermath, travel, reflection, and mundane activity that give a story its characteristic rhythm. A genre's pacing signature is as much about how it *breathes* as how it *strikes*.

Consider:
- **Approach beats** — the buildup before intensity. How does this genre characteristically approach its peaks? Through mounting dread, through accelerating complication, through deceptive calm?
- **Recovery beats** — the aftermath of intensity. How does this genre process what just happened? Through silence, through ritual, through conversation, through physical activity?
- **Transit beats** — movement between dramatic locations or phases. Does the genre treat transit as dead time, as opportunity for character development, as a source of its own tension?
- **Ambient beats** — scenes that establish mood, deepen setting, or let characters exist outside the plot. How much space does this genre give to simply *being* rather than *doing*?

## How to Find Shapes

- The genre's **temporal orientation** is the primary driver. Cyclical genres produce looping shapes; linear genres produce progressive shapes; episodic genres produce modular shapes.
- The genre's **agency model** determines whether the protagonist drives the arc or is driven by it. High-agency arcs have active turning points; low-agency arcs have revelatory turning points.
- The genre's **exclusions** constrain which resolutions are available. A genre that forbids catharsis produces shapes that must find alternative endpoints.
- The genre's **tonal contract** determines the emotional arc. A genre committed to dread cannot have a shape that resolves into relief without violating its contract.
- The genre's **state variables** create natural arc structures: variables that deplete create countdown shapes; variables that accumulate create pressure-cooker shapes; variables that oscillate create wave shapes.
- The genre's **ontological posture** shapes whether the arc involves transformation of the self, encounter with the Other, or negotiation between modes of being.
