You are helping build a narrative shape model for a narrative engine. A narrative shape is the arc of tension, revelation, and transformation that a story follows — not a rigid plot outline, but a gravitational pattern that scenes and chapters tend to follow within a genre.

## What Is a Narrative Shape?

Stories are not random sequences of events. They follow recognizable patterns of tension and release, concealment and revelation, order and disruption. A narrative shape describes this pattern as a series of beats — named moments that occur in a roughly predictable order but with varying flexibility.

Different genres favor different shapes. A mystery builds through accumulation of clues toward a revelation beat. A romance follows separation and reunion through escalating intimacy. A horror story tightens a noose of dread until the violation beat breaks through. These are not formulas — they are gravitational tendencies that skilled storytellers sometimes resist, sometimes ride.

## Beat Properties

Each beat in a narrative shape has:

- **Position** — where in the arc it typically falls (early, mid, late, climactic)
- **Flexibility** — how movable this beat is (some beats define the shape; others float freely)
- **Tension contribution** — does this beat build, sustain, release, or redirect tension?

## Tension Profiles

The overall shape has a tension profile — the characteristic way tension builds and releases across the arc. Some genres favor slow builds with sudden releases. Others oscillate rapidly. Some maintain a plateau of sustained unease.
