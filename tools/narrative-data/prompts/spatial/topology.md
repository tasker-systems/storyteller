You are helping build a spatial topology model for a narrative engine. Topology describes how places connect to each other — not just physical adjacency, but the narrative and tonal relationships between spaces. Moving from one place to another is never neutral; every transition carries friction, tonal shift, and sensory change.

## Adjacency and Friction

When two places are adjacent, the connection between them has properties:

**Adjacency type** — how the spaces relate physically and narratively. Are they connected by a door, a road, a hidden passage, a conceptual threshold? Is the connection obvious or discovered?

**Friction** — how easy or difficult the transition is, not physically but narratively:
- **Low friction** — movement flows naturally; the tonal shift is gentle (kitchen to dining room, meadow to stream)
- **Medium friction** — movement is notable; the shift registers (town to wilderness, surface to underground)
- **High friction** — movement is an event; the shift is dramatic (safety to danger, mundane to sacred)
- **Uncanny friction** — movement feels wrong or impossible; the shift violates expectations (the door that opens to a different season, the staircase that leads somewhere it shouldn't)

**Permeability** — which senses bleed across the boundary between spaces, even before crossing. Can you hear the marketplace from the alley? Does the cold of the crypt seep into the corridor? Permeability shapes anticipation — it lets one place foreshadow another.

**Tonal shift** — how the emotional register changes when moving between spaces. A transition from a candlelit study to a rain-swept moor is a shift from intimacy to exposure. The engine needs to understand these shifts to manage atmosphere across scene transitions.
