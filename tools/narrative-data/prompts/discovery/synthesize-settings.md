## What You Are Working With

Each genre extraction below identifies 5-8 settings or spatial environments grounded in that genre's specific dimensional properties. Some settings will appear across multiple genres with different names but similar spatial-atmospheric structures. Others will be genuinely unique to a single genre. Your job is to distinguish between these cases and produce a unified setting vocabulary for the cluster.

## Setting Dimensions (Reference)

Settings exist along continuous dimensions that describe their atmospheric and narrative texture:

- **Atmospheric signature** — welcoming/safe <-> hostile/threatening
- **Sensory dominance** — which senses the setting privileges (visual, auditory, tactile, olfactory, proprioceptive)
- **Temporal character** — frozen/timeless <-> dynamic/decaying
- **Spatial orientation** — enclosed/intimate <-> vast/exposed
- **Narrative permeability** — sealed/isolated <-> porous/connected
- **Symbolic density** — functional/literal <-> saturated/metaphorical
- **Communicability** — inert/backdrop <-> active/participant

## Your Task

Synthesize the extractions below into **8 to 12 distinct {primitive_type}** for the {cluster_name} cluster.

For each setting, provide:

1. **Canonical name** — a single descriptive label that works across genres. This should be evocative but not genre-locked. If the setting was called "the house that remembers" in one genre and "the ancestral seat of power" in another, find the name that captures the shared spatial-atmospheric pattern.

2. **Core identity** — 2-3 sentences describing this setting in genre-agnostic terms. What is the fundamental spatial-atmospheric shape? What atmospheric signature, narrative function, and communicability profile define it regardless of genre? This should be recognizable to someone who has never read the genre-specific extractions.

3. **Genre variants** — for each genre that produced a version of this setting, describe how that genre shifts the setting's expression. Which setting dimensions move, and in what direction? What about the genre's locus of power, temporal orientation, or aesthetic register changes how this setting feels and functions? Use specific dimensional language (e.g., "in folk horror, Communicability shifts from backdrop to active participant because the genre's locus of power resides in place itself — the landscape is not where things happen but the thing that happens to you").

4. **Uniqueness assessment** — classify as one of:
   - **Genre-unique**: Only one genre in this cluster produces this setting, and the structural reasons are specific to that genre's dimensional configuration
   - **Cluster-wide**: Multiple genres in this cluster produce variants, suggesting the cluster's shared properties give rise to it
   - **Likely universal**: This setting probably appears across most genre clusters, though with different atmospheric textures and narrative functions

## Synthesis Guidelines

**Merging**: Where multiple genres produced variants of what is structurally the same setting under different names, merge them. Preserve the genre-specific expressions as variants rather than discarding them. The goal is to recognize that "the abandoned manor," "the house with too many rooms," and "the estate that outlived its family" may all be the same setting in different genre clothing — defined by the intersection of enclosed space, temporal decay, and high symbolic density.

**Splitting**: Where two settings look superficially similar but occupy genuinely different positions in setting-dimension space, keep them separate. Explain the discriminating dimension. "The dark forest" and "the wilderness beyond the map" may both be outdoor threatening spaces, but they differ on the Narrative Permeability axis — the dark forest is a contained passage (you enter and exit), while the wilderness beyond the map is boundless and exit is uncertain. These create fundamentally different spatial experiences.

**Flagging provenance**: For each setting, note which genres contributed to it. If a setting emerges from only one genre, it may be genuinely genre-unique — or it may be that other genres produce it under a name you did not recognize as equivalent. State your confidence.

**Preserving richness**: Do not flatten the genre-specific detail into generic summaries. The value of this synthesis is precisely in the dimensional specificity — how the same spatial-atmospheric pattern shifts along measurable axes depending on genre context.
