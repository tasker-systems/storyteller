## What You Are Working With

Each genre extraction below identifies 5-8 interpersonal dynamics grounded in that genre's specific dimensional properties. Your job is to distinguish between these cases and produce a unified dynamics vocabulary for the cluster.

## Relational Dimensions (Reference)

Dynamics exist along continuous dimensions that describe the texture of interaction between two roles:

- **Power asymmetry** — balanced partnership <-> steep hierarchy
- **Symmetry** — reciprocal/mirrored <-> fundamentally asymmetric
- **Volatility** — stable/predictable <-> explosive/unstable
- **Dependency** — autonomous/optional <-> enmeshed/necessary
- **Visibility** — overt/acknowledged <-> covert/denied
- **Emotional register** — cold/transactional <-> warm/intimate
- **Evolution tendency** — crystallizing/static <-> transforming/unstable

## Your Task

Synthesize the extractions below into **8 to 12 distinct {primitive_type}** for the {cluster_name} cluster.

For each dynamic, provide:

1. **Canonical name** — a single descriptive label that works across genres. This should be evocative but not genre-locked. If the dynamic was called "the debt that compounds in silence" in one genre and "the unspoken ledger" in another, find the name that captures the shared relational pattern.

2. **Core identity** — 2-3 sentences describing this dynamic in genre-agnostic terms. What is the fundamental relational shape? What role definitions, power configuration, and evolution pattern define it regardless of genre? This should be recognizable to someone who has never read the genre-specific extractions.

3. **Genre variants** — for each genre that produced a version of this dynamic, describe how that genre shifts the dynamic's expression. Which relational dimensions move, and in what direction? What about the genre's locus of power, temporal orientation, or state variables changes how this dynamic plays out? Use specific dimensional language (e.g., "in noir, Volatility increases sharply because the genre's epistemological stance means either party may be operating on false information, making the dynamic perpetually unstable").

4. **Uniqueness assessment** — classify as one of:
   - **Genre-unique**: Only one genre in this cluster produces this dynamic, and the structural reasons are specific to that genre's dimensional configuration
   - **Cluster-wide**: Multiple genres in this cluster produce variants, suggesting the cluster's shared properties give rise to it
   - **Likely universal**: This dynamic probably appears across most genre clusters, though with different relational textures

## Synthesis Guidelines

**Merging**: Where multiple genres produced variants of what is structurally the same dynamic under different names, merge them. Preserve the genre-specific expressions as variants rather than discarding them. The goal is to recognize that "the alliance of necessity" and "the partnership neither wanted" and "the bond forged by shared threat" may all be the same dynamic in different genre clothing — defined by the intersection of high dependency and low initial affinity.

**Splitting**: Where two dynamics look superficially similar but occupy genuinely different positions in relational-dimension space, keep them separate. Explain the discriminating dimension. "Mentor/student" and "master/servant" may involve similar power asymmetry but differ fundamentally on the Dependency and Evolution axes — one aims at eventual independence, the other at perpetual service. These are different dynamics.

**Flagging provenance**: For each dynamic, note which genres contributed to it. If a dynamic emerges from only one genre, it may be genuinely genre-unique — or it may be that other genres produce it under a name you did not recognize as equivalent. State your confidence.

**Preserving richness**: Do not flatten the genre-specific detail into generic summaries. The value of this synthesis is precisely in the dimensional specificity — how the same relational pattern shifts along measurable axes depending on genre context.
