## What We Mean by Archetype Dynamics

An archetype dynamic is not a generic relationship (mentor-student, rivals, lovers). It is the specific dramatic tension that arises when two genre-grounded archetypes — each shaped by the genre's axes, each carrying their own distinguishing tensions — occupy the same narrative space. The tension is not invented; it is *implied* by the intersection of what each archetype needs, withholds, and represents within the genre's physics.

The Earnest Warden and the Unwilling Vessel in folk horror don't have a "guard-prisoner" dynamic. They have a dynamic defined by the fact that the Warden's warmth is genuine AND the Warden's duty demands the Vessel's sacrifice — and the Vessel can feel the warmth and therefore cannot simply hate the Warden. The genre's axes (earnestness, locus of power in the Land, knowledge as punishment) produce this specific relational shape. No other genre produces exactly this tension with these characters.

## How to Find Pairings

Do not enumerate all N×N combinations. Instead, look for pairings where:

- **Axis positions create productive opposition** — one archetype sits high on an axis where the other sits low, and the genre's physics make this opposition load-bearing rather than incidental
- **Obligations create asymmetric dependency** — one archetype needs something the other controls, but the cost of obtaining it transforms both parties
- **The genre's ontological posture creates boundary friction** — pairings where one archetype represents the centered subject and the other represents a being at the genre's self/Other boundary
- **The genre's narrative shapes place them in structural roles** — the narrative shape data for this genre implies certain characters must meet at certain beats. Which archetype pairings are structurally required by the genre's characteristic arcs?
- **The pairing produces a scene that defines the genre** — some genres are *known for* a particular kind of scene (the ritual, the confession, the heist, the duel). Which archetype pairing produces that signature scene?
- **Cross-scale tension emerges** — the orbital relationship between the archetypes is stable, but the arc-level events force it into motion, and the scene-level texture is where the friction is felt

Be expansive. This is exploratory research, not a database entry.
