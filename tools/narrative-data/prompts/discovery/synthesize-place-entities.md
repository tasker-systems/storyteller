## Your Task

Synthesize the per-genre extractions into **8-12 distinct place-entity patterns** for this cluster. For each:

1. **Canonical entity name** — the best label for this place-entity type across the cluster
2. **Core identity** — 2-3 sentences describing what makes this kind of place an entity. What is its characteristic communicability? What does it *do* in the narrative?
3. **Genre variants** — how does this place-entity express differently across the cluster? Note:
   - Communicability profile shifts (atmospheric-dominant in one genre, temporal-dominant in another)
   - State variable expression differences (what the place externalizes)
   - Ontological status differences (agent in one genre, backdrop in another — these differences are analytically valuable)
   - Evolution differences (does the place change across the arc?)
4. **Communicability signature** — what is the cluster-level communicability pattern? Which channels are always active? Which are genre-dependent?
5. **Topological role** — is this entity typically a hub, an endpoint, a threshold, or a sanctuary? Does the role shift by genre?
6. **Uniqueness** — cluster-specific, genre-unique, or likely universal?

## Merging and Splitting Guidelines

- **Merge** when two genres produce the same spatial being with different names (the domestic center is The Anchor Hearth in cozy fantasy and The Ledger Kitchen in realism — same topological role, different communicability)
- **Keep separate** when two place-entities occupy the same physical type but have different ontological status (a forest that is an agent vs. a forest that is an obstacle)
- **Flag** place-entities that are universal across the cluster — every genre in the cluster needs this kind of place
- **Flag** place-entities whose communicability changes during the narrative (the sanctuary that fails, the hostile place that becomes familiar)
