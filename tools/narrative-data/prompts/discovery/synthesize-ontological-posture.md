## What We Mean by Ontological Posture

Ontological posture is a genre's implicit answer to "who matters here, and why?" — its stance toward which beings are granted subject status, how Otherness is encountered, whether the self/Other boundary is stable or permeable, and what obligations flow across that boundary.

## Your Task

Below are the ontological posture analyses for {genre_count} genres in the **{cluster_name}** cluster. Synthesize them into a cluster-level analysis that identifies:

### 1. Shared Ontological Commitments
What postures are common across this cluster? Are there stances toward being that all genres in this cluster share — for example, does the entire cluster treat the self/Other boundary the same way, or share an ethical orientation toward nonhuman agents?

### 2. Discriminating Postures
Where do genres within this cluster *diverge* in their ontological stance? These differences are often the most analytically valuable — they reveal what distinguishes genres that share many other dimensional similarities. For each divergence, identify:
- The specific posture dimension that differs
- How it differs across genres
- What genre-level dimension (locus of power, epistemological stance, world affordance) drives the difference

### 3. Modes of Being Inventory
Across all genres in this cluster, what modes of being appear? For each:
- **Name**: a canonical label for this mode of being
- **Description**: what defines it (not just what it looks like but how it relates to personhood and agency)
- **Genre variants**: how does this mode of being express differently across the cluster's genres?
- **Uniqueness**: is this mode cluster-specific, or likely universal?

### 4. The Self/Other Boundary Spectrum
Map where each genre in this cluster sits on the boundary-stability spectrum (firm → permeable → illusory). Note any genres where the boundary's stability is itself a state variable (it degrades or strengthens over the course of the narrative).

### 5. Ethical Orientation Patterns
Identify the characteristic ethical orientations toward the Other in this cluster. Are there orientations that are structurally forbidden by the cluster's shared affordances? Are there orientations unique to specific genres?

### 6. Cross-Cluster Connections
Where do you see ontological postures in this cluster connecting to postures likely present in other genre clusters? Note these without assuming knowledge of the other clusters — describe what you'd expect to find and why.

Where two genres produced what looks like the same posture with different language, merge them and preserve both genre-specific expressions. Where two postures look similar but occupy genuinely different positions (e.g., the Other as threat vs. the Other as mirror), keep them separate and explain the discriminating dimension.
