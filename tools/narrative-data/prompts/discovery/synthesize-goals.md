## Your Task

Synthesize the per-genre extractions into **10-15 distinct goals** for this cluster. For each:

1. **Canonical name** — the best label for this goal across the cluster's genres
2. **Scale** — existential, arc, scene, or cross-scale? Note if the same goal operates at different scales in different genres
3. **Core identity** — 2-3 sentences describing what defines this goal genre-independently. What hunger does it express? What is the character reaching for?
4. **Genre variants** — how does this goal express differently across the cluster's genres? Note:
   - Scale shifts (existential in one genre, arc-level in another — this tells you something about how seriously the genre takes this motivation)
   - Success accessibility shifts (achievable in some genres, structurally tragic in others)
   - Moral weight shifts (neutral in some genres, fraught in others)
   - Whether the goal involves nonhuman entities in some genres
5. **Cross-scale tension patterns** — how does this goal create friction between temporal scales? Where pursuing the scene goal undermines the arc goal, or achieving the arc goal costs the existential goal — these patterns are the most narratively productive. Note which genres produce which tension patterns.
6. **State variable connections** — which canonical state variables (psychological_integrity, social_standing, bond_trust, etc.) does this goal interact with? How does the interaction differ by genre?
7. **Uniqueness** — cluster-specific, genre-unique, or likely universal?

## Merging and Splitting Guidelines

- **Merge** when two genres produce the same motivational structure under different names at the same scale. Preserve both genre-specific expressions.
- **Keep separate** when two goals look similar but operate at different scales (existential dignity is structurally different from arc-level "win respect" even if both involve being valued).
- **Keep separate** when two goals interact with different state variables or produce different cross-scale tensions.
- **Flag** goals that are structurally unattainable in specific genres — these are genre-defining. The impossibility is the point. (Escape in folk horror. Individual victory in working-class realism. Full knowledge in cosmic horror.)
- **Flag** goals that derive from or connect to specific archetypes already identified for this cluster — the Earnest Warden's goal of maintaining the pact, the Hearthwarden's goal of sanctuary integrity.
