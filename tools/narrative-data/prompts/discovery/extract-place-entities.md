## What We Mean by Place-Entity

A place-entity is not a location description. It is a place that has crossed the communicability threshold — a space with sufficient atmospheric, sensory, spatial, and temporal presence to participate in the narrative as an agent. Not every place becomes an entity; a corridor may be a transition, not a being. But the places that define a genre — the places where the genre's physics are most visible — are entities.

The Throat of the Land in folk horror is not "a well in a village." It is an entity that communicates through the wrongness of its water, that holds secrets in its depth, that demands something of anyone who draws from it. The Ledger Kitchen in working-class realism is not "a kitchen with bills on the table." It is an entity whose cleared or cluttered table *is* the state variable of financial solvency, whose light quality communicates exhaustion or hope, whose rhythms of cooking and cleaning are the rhythms of survival.

The distinction: a location has properties. A place-entity has **communicability** — it sends signals, it resists or yields, it changes in response to what happens within it, and it shapes what characters can do and feel.

## Communicability Dimensions

Every place-entity communicates along four channels:

- **Atmospheric** — the mood the place generates. Not described by characters but *felt* by them and communicated to the reader through sensory detail. The atmospheric channel is what makes a reader uneasy before any character has spoken.
- **Sensory** — what the place offers to the senses. Dominant and recessive modalities. A place dominated by sound (dripping water, creaking wood) communicates differently than one dominated by light (harsh fluorescent, filtered sunlight). Sensory channels carry information — the smell of fresh paint says something different than the smell of rot.
- **Spatial** — how the place structures movement and proximity. Compressed spaces force intimacy. Vast spaces create isolation. Vertical spaces create hierarchy. The spatial channel constrains what kinds of scenes can happen here.
- **Temporal** — how time feels in this place. Some places freeze time (the waiting room). Some accelerate it (the deadline office). Some loop it (the seasonal kitchen). The temporal channel connects to the genre's pacing and narrative shape.

## How to Find Place-Entities

- Start from the **spatial topology** — the transitions with the highest friction or tonal inheritance are connected to places with the highest communicability.
- Look at the genre's **ontological posture** — which places does this genre grant agency to? Those are the place-entities.
- Look at the genre's **narrative shapes** — which places are where the load-bearing beats happen? Those places are entities because they *must* participate in the drama.
- Look at the genre's **state variables** — which places externalize state? A place that changes its appearance as a variable shifts is communicating through its atmospheric and sensory channels.
- The genre's **settings data** already names candidates — the settings that were described with atmospheric palettes, narrative functions, and communicability dimensions are the most likely place-entities.
