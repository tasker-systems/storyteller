## What We Mean by Setting

A setting is not a backdrop or a location label. It is a recurring spatial-atmospheric pattern that emerges because a genre's specific constraints, affordances, and thematic commitments make that kind of place structurally necessary or uniquely possible. The "library where knowledge is dangerous" arises in cosmic horror because the genre's epistemological stance treats understanding as corrosive and its locus of power resides in forbidden texts. The "kitchen where community happens" arises in cozy fantasy because the genre's warmth axis is high and its state variables track belonging through shared domestic ritual.

We are not looking for conventional location lists. We are looking for the spatial-atmospheric patterns that this genre's physics demand.

## Setting Dimensions (Reference)

Settings exist along continuous dimensions that describe their atmospheric and narrative texture:

- **Atmospheric signature** — welcoming/safe <-> hostile/threatening. What is the characteristic mood this setting type creates? Is it comforting, unsettling, sublime, oppressive, or liminal?
- **Sensory dominance** — which senses does this setting type privilege? Visual, auditory, tactile, olfactory, proprioceptive? What sensory details define the experience of being in this place?
- **Temporal character** — frozen/timeless <-> dynamic/decaying. How does time feel in this setting? Is it a place where time pools, accelerates, or stops mattering?
- **Spatial orientation** — enclosed/intimate <-> vast/exposed. Does this setting type create compression or expansion? Claustrophobia or agoraphobia? Containment or freedom?
- **Narrative permeability** — sealed/isolated <-> porous/connected. Can the outside world intrude, or is this a sealed container? Can characters leave freely?
- **Symbolic density** — functional/literal <-> saturated/metaphorical. Is this setting primarily a physical space, or does it carry heavy symbolic weight? Does the architecture literalize psychological states?
- **Communicability** — inert/backdrop <-> active/participant. Does this setting merely contain the story, or does it act upon characters, constrain choices, and generate events?

## How to Find Settings

Look beyond the obvious. Examine:

- The genre's **locus of power** — where power resides determines what kinds of spaces matter. If power is in place itself, settings are not backdrops but agents. If power is in lineage, settings that embody family history (ancestral homes, inherited lands) become structurally essential. If power is in knowledge, settings organized around information (libraries, observatories, interrogation rooms) are necessary.
- The genre's **exclusions** — what settings are forbidden or structurally impossible? If a genre rejects safety, then settings that traditionally provide refuge must be transformed or subverted. The boundary of what's excluded reveals which spatial patterns are essential.
- The genre's **temporal orientation** — cyclical genres produce settings that characters return to transformed; linear genres produce settings that are encountered once and left behind. The same farmhouse means something different in a genre of seasonal return versus a genre of permanent departure.
- The genre's **state variables** — what resources are tracked in relation to space? If the genre tracks isolation, settings organize along an axis of connection to the broader world. If it tracks safety, settings organize along an axis of vulnerability and shelter.
- The genre's **aesthetic register** — how the genre renders physical space determines which settings it can use effectively. A genre that privileges sensory detail produces settings rich in texture. A genre that privileges interiority produces settings that mirror psychological states.
- The genre's **world affordances** — the physics of the genre world (magic systems, technology, supernatural presence, violence consequences) determine what settings are materially possible and what they can do.

Any spatial-atmospheric pattern that sits at a distinct intersection of genre axes — especially where communicability, narrative function, and symbolic density create a distinctive place-identity — is likely a setting worth naming.
