## What We Mean by Profile

A profile is not a plot beat or a narrative event. It is a recurring scene shape — a pattern of dramatic situation, tension, and resolution that emerges because a genre's specific constraints and affordances make that kind of scene structurally necessary or uniquely possible. The "interrogation where both parties lie" arises in noir because the genre's epistemological stance treats all information as suspect and its locus of power resides in who controls the conversation. The "quiet meal after catastrophe" arises in literary fiction because the genre's temporal dynamics privilege the aftermath over the event.

We are not looking for conventional plot points. We are looking for the scene shapes that this genre's physics demand.

## Scene Dimensions (Reference)

Scenes exist along continuous dimensions that describe their dramatic texture and structural function:

- **Tension signature** — ambient/sustained <-> spiking/explosive. How does tension behave within this scene type? Does it build, plateau, oscillate, or rupture?
- **Pacing** — slow/atmospheric <-> rapid/kinetic. What is the characteristic rhythm? How does time feel inside this scene?
- **Cast density** — intimate/few characters <-> ensemble/crowded. How many voices does this scene type typically require?
- **Information flow** — withholding/accumulating <-> revealing/spending. Does this scene type hide or disclose? Does it create mysteries or resolve them?
- **Emotional register** — restrained/intellectual <-> visceral/overwhelming. What is the characteristic emotional temperature?
- **Resolution tendency** — closed/conclusive <-> open/suspended. Does this scene type resolve its central tension, defer it, or transform it into something else?
- **Physical dynamism** — static/contained <-> mobile/expansive. Does this scene type stay in one place, or does it move through space?

## How to Find Profiles

Look beyond the obvious. Examine:

- The genre's **tension signature** — how does tension characteristically behave? Genres that sustain ambient dread produce different scene types than genres that spike and release. The characteristic tension pattern determines what scenes feel native.
- The genre's **exclusions** — what scene types are forbidden? If a genre rejects catharsis, then the scene shapes it produces must find alternative forms of emotional resolution. The boundary of what's excluded reveals what's structurally essential.
- The genre's **locus of power** — where power resides determines what kinds of scenes organize around it. If power is in conversation, the genre produces elaborate dialogue scenes. If power is in place, the genre produces scenes of arrival, exploration, and territorial contest.
- The genre's **temporal orientation** — cyclical genres produce scenes of ritual and return; linear genres produce scenes of irreversible consequence. A feast scene in a cyclical genre is a seasonal marker; in a linear genre, it may be the last gathering before everything changes.
- The genre's **state variables** — what resources are tracked or depleted in scenes? If the genre tracks information, scenes organize around disclosure and concealment. If it tracks safety, scenes organize around threat and shelter.
- The genre's **aesthetic register** — how the genre renders experience determines the texture of its scenes. A genre that privileges interiority produces scenes built around internal experience. A genre that privileges action produces scenes built around physical consequence.

- The genre's **ontological posture** — who and what counts as a being in this genre shapes what scenes are possible. If the genre grants agency to the environment, then scenes of negotiation with the Land, communion with the Cosmos, or confrontation with the System become structurally available. Consider scenes where nonhuman agents participate as full dramatic partners, not just backdrop. A scene where "the house remembers" is a different dramatic shape than a scene where a character remembers in a house.

Any dramatic situation that sits at a distinct intersection of genre axes — especially where tension signature, information flow, and emotional register create a distinctive scene shape — is likely a profile worth naming.
